 National Pension System (NPS) is a voluntary, long-term retirement savings scheme designed to enable systematic savings during your working life. It is regulated by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA).

**Eligibility:**
- Any citizen of India (resident or non-resident) between 18 and 70 years of age can open an NPS account.
- Overseas Citizens of India (OCI) are also eligible to enroll in NPS.

**Account Types:**
1. **Tier I Account:**
   - This is the primary pension account and is mandatory.
   - Withdrawals are restricted and subject to specific conditions to ensure corpus accumulation for retirement.
   - Contributions to Tier I are eligible for tax deductions under Section 80C and Section 80CCD(1B) of the Income Tax Act.

2. **Tier II Account:**
   - This is a voluntary savings facility available as an add-on to any active Tier I account holder.
   - Subscribers can withdraw their savings from this account anytime, without restriction.
   - No tax benefits are available for contributions to Tier II accounts (except for government employees under specific conditions).

**Asset Classes:**
- **Equity (E):** Investments in stocks/shares. High risk, high return potential. Cap of 75%.
- **Corporate Bonds (C):** Investments in fixed income instruments issued by private companies. Moderate risk.
- **Government Securities (G):** Investments in government bonds. Low risk.
- **Alternative Assets (A):** Investments in REITs, InvITs, etc. Cap of 5%.

**Investment Choices:**
- **Active Choice:** Individual decides the asset allocation (%) between E, C, G, and A.
- **Auto Choice (Lifecycle Fund):** Allocation is automatically adjusted based on age.
  - **LC75 (Aggressive):** Starts with 75% Equity up to age 35, then tapers.
  - **LC50 (Moderate):** Starts with 50% Equity up to age 35, then tapers.
  - **LC25 (Conservative):** Starts with 25% Equity up to age 35, then tapers.

**Exit & Withdrawal:**
- **At 60 Years:** At least 40% of the corpus must be used to purchase an annuity (monthly pension). The remaining 60% can be withdrawn as a tax-free lump sum.
- **Before 60 Years (Premature Exit):** Allowed only after 10 years. At least 80% must be used for annuity; max 20% lump sum.
- **Partial Withdrawal:** Up to 25% of own contributions allowed after 3 years for specific reasons (illness, education, housing). Max 3 times in tenure.
